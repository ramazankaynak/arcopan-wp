## Frequently Asked Questions

### Cold Storage Design & Engineering

**Q: What is the typical timeline for cold storage facility design and installation?**

A: Timeline varies based on complexity and facility size:

- **Simple chilled room (100–200m²):** 8–12 weeks total (2 weeks design, 6–8 weeks construction)
- **Medium facility (500–1,000m²):** 12–16 weeks total
- **Large facility (2,000–5,000m²):** 16–24 weeks total
- **Mega-scale (10,000m²+):** 24–36 weeks total

Most timeline is construction and equipment delivery. Engineering design typically requires 2–4 weeks. Project-based customs systems may require 2–3 weeks additional design time.

**Q: How do we calculate the refrigeration capacity needed?**

A: Refrigeration capacity is determined by:

1. **Thermal Load Calculation:** Heat transfer through walls, roof, floor based on U-values and temperature difference
2. **Product Load:** Heat from product being cooled/stored (varies with product type and volume)
3. **Infiltration Load:** Heat from door openings and air infiltration
4. **Safety Margin:** 15–25% additional capacity for peak loads and climate variations

Our engineers calculate exact capacity using thermal simulation software. Industry rule-of-thumb: ~30–50 W per m³ for chilled storage, ~50–80 W per m³ for frozen storage.

**Q: What insulation thickness do we need?**

A: Insulation thickness depends on:

- **Temperature Differential:** Larger temperature differences require thicker insulation (e.g., -25°C requires thicker than +4°C)
- **Climate:** Hotter ambient climates require more insulation
- **Operating Hours:** 24/7 facilities benefit from more insulation than seasonal facilities
- **Budget:** More insulation = higher capital cost but lower operating costs

Typical recommendations:
- Chilled (0/+4°C): 100–150mm PIR
- Frozen (-18/-25°C): 150–200mm PIR
- Blast freezing (-35°C): 200–250mm PIR

Final specification comes from thermal load calculations.

**Q: Do we need separate refrigeration circuits for chilled and frozen zones?**

A: Yes, typically. Separate circuits enable:

- Independent temperature control in each zone
- Flexibility if one circuit fails (other zone still operational)
- Efficiency (can shut down unused zones)

Some small facilities use a single circuit with multiple thermostats, but separate circuits are preferred for reliability and control.

### Products & Specifications

**Q: What does "U-value" mean and why does it matter?**

A: U-value measures thermal transmittance (how quickly heat passes through insulation). Lower U-values are better:

- **U = 0.20 W/m²K:** Good insulation (standard chilled storage)
- **U = 0.15 W/m²K:** Excellent insulation (frozen storage)
- **U = 0.10 W/m²K:** Premium insulation (ultra-low temperature or energy-critical)

Better insulation (lower U-value) reduces operating costs 20–30% over facility lifetime, often justifying higher capital cost.

**Q: What core material should we use: PIR, PUR, or XPS?**

A: Common comparison:

- **PIR (Polyisocyanurate):** Best thermal performance, superior fire rating, most durable. Recommended for food and pharmaceutical.
- **PUR (Polyurethane):** Good thermal performance, cost-effective, acceptable for most applications.
- **XPS (Extruded Polystyrene):** Adequate for basic applications, lower cost, not recommended for high-performance facilities.

ARCOPAN typically recommends PIR for cold storage due to superior performance and fire safety.

**Q: What's the typical lifespan of insulated panels?**

A: Quality insulated panels (PIR-core) typically last 20–30 years in cold storage service. Factors affecting lifespan:

- **Material quality:** Premium PIR outlasts commodity foam
- **Installation quality:** Proper sealing prevents degradation
- **Operating environment:** Stable temperatures extend life; temperature cycling reduces it
- **Maintenance:** Regular inspection and repair extends lifespan

Panel degradation is typically slow. Most facilities remain functional for 25+ years if properly maintained.

### Food Safety & Compliance

**Q: How do we maintain HACCP compliance?**

A: HACCP compliance requires:

1. **Documentation:** Defined cold storage procedures and critical control points
2. **Temperature Monitoring:** Regular temperature recording and documentation
3. **System Maintenance:** Regular equipment maintenance and testing
4. **Staff Training:** Employee training on procedures and alarm response
5. **Audit Trail:** Records of all temperature excursions and corrective actions

ARCOPAN facilities are HACCP-designed and include documentation support.

**Q: What's required for pharmaceutical GDP compliance?**

A: GDP (Good Distribution Practice) for pharmaceuticals requires:

1. **Temperature Precision:** ±1°C accuracy with continuous monitoring
2. **Audit Trail:** Every temperature reading logged and auditable
3. **Excursion Response:** Immediate notification and documented response to temperature excursions
4. **Personnel:** Trained personnel with documented responsibilities
5. **Documentation:** Complete facility records and inspection readiness

ARCOPAN provides GDP-certified cold storage with monitoring systems built to regulatory specification.

**Q: How often must we perform temperature mapping?**

A: Temperature mapping should be performed:

- **Initial commissioning:** Before handover to verify system meets specification
- **Annually:** To verify continued accuracy across facility
- **After major repair:** If major component (compressor, evaporator) is replaced
- **Following excursion:** To verify integrity after temperature alarm

Mapping involves placing temperature probes throughout facility and recording temperatures over 24–48 hour period. Typically performed annually by external testing firm.

### Operations & Maintenance

**Q: What is regular maintenance for a cold storage facility?**

A: Typical maintenance schedule:

- **Daily:** Visual inspection, alarm system check, product inspection
- **Weekly:** Temperature log review, door seal inspection, defrost cycle verification
- **Monthly:** Evaporator fan cleaning, condenser coil inspection, refrigerant charge verification
- **Quarterly:** Full system inspection, electrical safety check, backup alarm test
- **Annually:** Third-party service inspection, component testing, seals replacement if needed

Proper maintenance extends facility lifespan and prevents expensive emergency repairs.

**Q: How much does cold storage typically cost to operate?**

A: Operating cost estimates:

- **Electricity:** Primary cost driver. Expect €15,000–25,000/year per 1,000m² for 24/7 chilled storage; €25,000–40,000/year for frozen storage
- **Maintenance:** €3,000–5,000/year per 1,000m² for routine maintenance
- **Repairs:** €2,000–5,000/year for component replacement and repairs

Total operating cost roughly €5–8 per m³ per month. Optimized insulation and refrigeration can reduce costs 20–30%.

**Q: What should we do if there's a temperature excursion?**

A: Standard excursion response:

1. **Immediate:** Note time and temperature reading
2. **Investigation:** Determine cause (door left open, compressor failure, etc.)
3. **Correction:** Take immediate corrective action (close door, service compressor, etc.)
4. **Documentation:** Record excursion, cause, and corrective action
5. **Assessment:** Determine if product is safe or requires quarantine
6. **Communication:** Notify management and relevant parties (QA, sales, regulatory if required)

ARCOPAN facilities include alarm systems and monitoring to detect excursions immediately.

### Expansion & Upgrades

**Q: Can we expand a cold storage facility later?**

A: Yes, with considerations:

- **Space:** Physical space for additional panels and racking must be available
- **Refrigeration:** May need to upgrade compressor or add secondary circuit
- **Electrical:** Increased electrical load may require service upgrade
- **Layout:** Expansion should be planned during initial design if possible

ARCOPAN can design modular facilities that scale from 200m² to 2,000m²+ if expansion is anticipated.

**Q: What's involved in upgrading refrigeration equipment?**

A: Equipment upgrades typically require:

- **Design:** New equipment sizing and specification
- **Procurement:** 4–8 weeks lead time for industrial equipment
- **Installation:** 1–3 weeks installation and commissioning
- **Testing:** Temperature mapping and system verification

Upgrades can typically occur without shutting down facility if done in phases (upgrade one circuit while other remains operational).

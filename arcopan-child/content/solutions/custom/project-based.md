## Introduction

Every industrial facility is unique. Your cold storage requirements may combine specialized temperature zones, unique logistics constraints, regulatory compliance demands, or integration with existing infrastructure that standard solution templates cannot address.

ARCOPAN's project-based systems deliver custom-engineered cold storage facilities designed specifically for your facility's unique requirements. We handle the complete engineering, procurement, and installation workflow — delivering a turnkey cold storage system built to your exact specification.

## When Project-Based Engineering Is Required

You need custom engineering when your project has:

- **Specialized Temperature Requirements:** Multi-zone facilities, ultra-low blast freezing, or precision pharmaceutical storage
- **Integration Complexity:** Cold storage integration with existing production facilities, automation systems, or supply chain infrastructure
- **Regulatory Demands:** GDP/GMP compliance, ATEX hazardous atmosphere certification, or specialized food safety requirements
- **Unique Logistics:** Non-standard dock design, custom racking configurations, or specialized material handling
- **Facility Constraints:** Retrofit into existing buildings, tight space utilization, or structural engineering requirements
- **Future Expansion:** Phased rollout strategy, modular design for capacity scaling, or future technology upgrades

## The ARCOPAN Project Delivery Model

Project-based systems follow a structured engineering and delivery process:

### Phase 1: Feasibility & Engineering
**Duration:** 2–4 weeks
We conduct detailed requirements analysis, thermal load calculations, refrigeration design, and facility layout optimization. Deliverables: Preliminary design, budget estimate, timeline, and risk assessment.

### Phase 2: Detailed Design & Procurement
**Duration:** 4–8 weeks
Full engineering drawings, equipment specifications, and material procurement. All suppliers and contractors confirmed. Final budget locked. Delivery timeline finalized.

### Phase 3: Manufacturing & Logistics
**Duration:** 8–16 weeks
Panel manufacturing, equipment fabrication, logistics coordination. On-site prep work begins. Contractor teams mobilized.

### Phase 4: Installation & Commissioning
**Duration:** 4–12 weeks (facility dependent)
Full installation supervision, refrigerant charging, temperature mapping, system testing, and operator training. Final handover with full documentation.
